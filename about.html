ABOUT THE UNITED STATES

Learn about our nation, symbols, and capital

The United States of America

The United States of America (USA) is a prominent nation located in North America, comprising 50 states and a federal district, with Washington, D.C. serving as the capital. Recognized globally for its economic strength, technological advancements, and cultural influence, the USA plays a significant role in international affairs. The country is characterized by its diverse population and a wide range of geographic features, including mountains, forests, deserts, and coastlines. Its rich history and commitment to democratic values have shaped its development into one of the most influential nations in the world.

The American Flag

The flag of the United States of America, commonly known as the "Stars and Stripes," is a powerful national symbol. It consists of 13 horizontal stripes of equal width, alternating red and white, which represent the original 13 colonies that declared independence from Great Britain. In the upper left corner, there is a blue rectangle known as the canton or union, containing 50 white, five-pointed stars arranged in nine offset horizontal rows. These stars symbolize the 50 states that make up the United States today. The colors of the flag also hold meaning: red signifies valor and bravery, white symbolizes purity and innocence, and blue represents vigilance, perseverance, and justice. The design of the flag reflects both the country's history and its unity as a nation.

Washington, D.C.

Washington, D.C., the capital of the United States, is located on the east coast between Maryland and Virginia. It serves as the center of the federal government, housing the White House, the U.S. Capitol, and the Supreme Court. The city is known for its historic monuments, including the Washington Monument and Lincoln Memorial, as well as world-renowned museums like those of the Smithsonian Institution. As the political heart of the nation, Washington, D.C. plays a key role in shaping U.S. laws, policies, and international relations.
